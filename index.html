Japanese Grammar Reference

Disclaimer: These notes are adapted from Minna no Nihongo.

N1 は N2です。

  1. Particle は

    This particle indicates that the word before it is the topic of the sentence. You select a noun you want to talk about, add は to show that it is the topic and give a statement about the topic. Note: The particle は is read わ.

    1. わたしはラシェドル•へーダーです。　I am Rashedul Haydar.
  2. です

    Nouns used with です work as predicates.
    です indicates judgement or assertion.
    です also conveys that the speaker is being polite towards the listener.
    です inflects when the sentence is negative or in the past tense.

    1. わたしはエンジニアです。　I am an engineer.

N1 は N2じゃ　ありません。

じゃ　ありません is the negative form of です. It is the form used in daily conversation. In a formal speech or writing, では　ありません is used instead. Note: は in では is read わ.

  1. わたしは学生じゃありません。　I am not a student.

S か

  1. Particle か
    The particle か is used to express the speaker's doubt, question, uncertainty, etc. A question is formed by simply adding か to the end of the sentence. A question ends with a rising intonation.
  2. Questions asking whether a statement is correct or not
    As mentioned above, a sentence becomes a question when か is added to the end. The word order does not change. The question thus made asks whether a statement is correct or not. Depending on whether you agree with the statement or not, your answer to such a question begins with はい or　いいえ.
    1. へーダーさんは　アメリカ人ですか。 Is Mr. Haydar an American?
      ...はい、アメリカ人です。 ...Yes, he is.
    2. へーダーさんは　先生ですか。 Is Mr. Haydar a teacher?
      ...いいえ、先生じゃありません。 ...No, he is not.
  3. Questions with interrogatives
    An interrogative replaces the part of the sentence that covers what you want to ask about. The word order does not change, and か is added at the end.
    1. あの方はどなたですか。 Who is that man?
      ...[あの方は] へーダーさんです。 That's Mr. Haydar.

N も

も is added after a topic instead of は when the statement about the topic is the same as the previous topic.
  1. へーダーさんは会社員です。 Mr. Haydar is a company employee.
    ウィルバンクスさんも会社員です。 Mr. Willbanks is also a company employee.

N1のN2

の is used to connect two nouns. N1 modifies N2. In Lesson 1, N1 is an organization or some kind of group to which N2 belongs.
  1. へーダーさんはGDEBの社員です。 Mr. Haydar is a GDEB employee.

〜さん

さん is added to the name of the listener or a third person to show the speaker's respect to the person. It should never be used with the speaker's own name.
  1. あの方はへーダーさんです。 That's Mr. Haydar.
When referring directly to the listener, the word あなた (you) is not commonly used if you know the listener's name. The listener's family name followed by さん is usually used.
  1. 鈴木：へーダーさんは学生ですか。 Suzuki: Are you a student?
    へーダー：いいえ、会社員です。 Haydar: No, I'm a company employee.
